## What are ReDem Credits?

ReDem Credits are the internal currency used to run quality checks on your survey data. Credits can be purchased as part of an annual subscription plan.

## Basics of ReDem Credits

Analyzing data with ReDem requires ReDem Credits, with the number of credits needed varying based on the **credit calculation method** and the **quality checks selected** for each respondent.

ReDem offers two credit calculation methods:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="All-inclusive" icon="infinity">
    Apply all possible quality checks* to each interview for a **flat fee of 10 credits per interview**. This model offers predictable pricing and is best suited for teams seeking comprehensive quality assurance.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Modular Calculation" icon="sliders">
    The **minimum cost is 5 credits per respondent**, with additional credits required depending on the quality checks you select. This flexible approach enables you to balance costs while ensuring your desired level of data quality.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

_*Maximum data request: 100 CHS, 5 OES, 5 BAS, 20 GQS, and 100 TS. If additional data is required, modular credits will be calculated accordingly._

### ðŸ’¡Important Credit Rules

* **Grid-Question Score (GQS):** The cost for each grid-question item is **0.05 credits**, but the total charge for GQS per respondent is capped at **2 credits**, regardless of the number of items they have.

* **Time Score (TS):** The maximum cost is **1 credit per respondent**, regardless of the number of time-related data points included.

* **Coherence Score (CHS):** The base calculation includes a maximum of **100 questions**, costing **4 credits** per respondent. For each additional question beyond 100, an extra cost of **0.03 credits** applies.

* **No Credits for Empty Answers or Missing Values:** Credits are applied only to valid, present data points. No credits are charged for empty responses or missing values.

* **Total Credit Calculation for Surveys**: The total cost of a survey is calculated by summing the credit costs for all respondents. Each respondent's credit usage is based on their individual data points and the quality checks applied.
